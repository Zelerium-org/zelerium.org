    Zelerium

    Zelerium is a digital currency that is currently in development, similar to Bitcoin. It's a form of virtual money that exists only online and can be used for buying things or trading with others.

    One key aspect of Zelerium, like Bitcoin, is its limited supply. There will only ever be 10,000,000 of Zelerium coins available. This scarcity contributes to its value.

    Closely similar to how people "mine" Bitcoin, individuals may have the opportunity to mine Zelerium. Mining involves validating transactions, and in return, miners are rewarded with Zelerium coins.

    Zelerium Proof of Stake

    Proof of Stake (PoS), like Proof of Work, is a blockchain consensus algorithm. Though it is remarkably different in the way it works. Many blockchains use this algorithm in production.

    In order to add new blocks to the blockchain, an algorithm is required to decide which miner gets the ability to add a block to the blockchain. Hence, this is the primary goal of any consensus algorithm.

    Working

    In Proof of Stake, miners are required to stake their balance in order to be chosen as the next block creator. Therefore, the miner that stakes the most amount of its currency has the highest chance of being chosen as the leader and creating the next block. Some blockchain solutions don't follow such principle and instead choose the miner on a random basis.

    If a miner is caught cheating or tampers with a block, it loses its entire staked coins. Hence, such a restriction compels the miners to be honest
